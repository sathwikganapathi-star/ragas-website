What are Ragas?

A Raga is a melodic framework used in Indian classical music. It consists of a set of musical notes (swaras) in a particular scale, with a specific order and emphasis on certain notes. Each Raga has its own unique personality, mood, and character.

Key Characteristics:

  • Arohana: The ascending notes of the raga
  • Avarohana: The descending notes of the raga
  • Vadi-Samvadi: The most and second most important notes
  • Pakad: The characteristic phrase or pattern
  • Time of Performance: Specific ragas for morning, evening, or night

Popular Ragas

Bhairav

Morning

Description: One of the most ancient ragas, Bhairav evokes devotion and spiritual awakening. It is full of energy and majesty.

Specialties:

  • Divine and devotional mood
  • Performed at sunrise
  • Uses all seven notes (shuddh swaras)
  • Creates a majestic atmosphere

Swaras: S R G M P D N S

Yaman

Evening

Description: Yaman is a light, cheerful, and romantic raga that brings joy and happiness. It's one of the most popular ragas in Hindustani music.

Specialties:

  • Romantic and joyful mood
  • Performed in the evening
  • Sharp fourth (Teevra Madhyam)
  • Beautiful and melodious

Swaras: S R G M' P D N S

Jor

Daytime

Description: Jor is a cheerful, energetic raga performed during daytime. It creates a sense of enthusiasm and positivity.

Specialties:

  • Energetic and cheerful atmosphere
  • Daytime performance
  • Bright and optimistic mood
  • Popular in classical concerts

Swaras: S R G P D N S

Rageshwari

Evening

Description: Rageshwari is a serene and devotional raga that creates a sense of peace and tranquility. It has a melancholic touch with spiritual depth.

Specialties:

  • Peaceful and spiritual mood
  • Evening performance
  • Meditative and calming
  • Rich in emotional content

Swaras: S R G M P D N S

Bhopali

Afternoon

Description: Bhopali is a pentatonic raga with a sweet and romantic character. It's simple yet deeply melodious and emotionally expressive.

Specialties:

  • Sweet and romantic mood
  • Afternoon performance
  • Uses only 5 notes (pentatonic)
  • Easy to learn but difficult to master

Swaras: S G P D N S

Marwa

Late Evening

Description: Marwa is a sophisticated and complex raga with a unique character. It evokes passion, longing, and depth of emotion.

Specialties:

  • Passionate and complex mood
  • Late evening performance
  • Characteristic ascending patterns
  • Requires skilled performance

Swaras: S R M' P D N S

Malkauns

Night

Description: Malkauns is a meditative and introspective raga performed late at night. It creates a mood of contemplation and mystery.

Specialties:

  • Meditative and mysterious mood
  • Night performance
  • Uses only 5 notes
  • Perfect for late-night concerts

Swaras: S G M' D N S

Kharaharapriya

Anytime

Description: Kharaharapriya is a versatile raga that can be performed anytime. It's known for its versatility and expressive potential.

Specialties:

  • Versatile and expressive
  • Can be performed anytime
  • Both devotional and secular forms
  • Rich scope for improvisation

Swaras: S R G M P D N' S

Two Major Systems of Ragas

Hindustani

Region: North India

Characteristics: More improvisational, emphasis on improvisation and flexibility. Uses Dhrupad, Khayal, Thumri forms.

Notable: Ragas like Yaman, Bhairav, and Jor are popular in this system.

Carnatic

Region: South India

Characteristics: More structured and systematic. Uses Kriti, Varnam, and Padam forms.

Notable: Ragas like Dheerashankarabharanam and Saveri are popular in this system.
